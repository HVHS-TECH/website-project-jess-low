Influenza

Overview:

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is an infectious disease. It is often spread by particles created when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes.


Types:

There are four main types of influenza: A, B, C, and D. Type A infects birds, humans, and some mammals. Type B is a less common strain and mainly infects humans, though it will occasionally infect other creatures. Type C also mainly infects humans and is milder than types A and B. Type D infects pigs and cattle.


Symptoms:

Influenza symptoms often include a fever, headache, runny nose, coughing, muscle pain, sore throat and occasionally diarrhoea or vomiting. However, it can be asymptomatic or partially asymptomatic, meaning you either never show symptoms or only show symptoms a few days after catching it. It typically lasts for a few days to a few weeks, but it can progress to pneumonia. It’s usually very rare to have fatalities, with the fatality rate being 0.1%.


Outbreaks:

There have been a few pandemics of varying severity, such as swine flu, Russian flu, Hong Kong flu, and Asian flu. However, the most memorable and severe of all was the Spanish flu. While being called the ‘Spanish’ flu, the name is misleading. Spain was the first to freely report the outbreak, giving it the name, but there are conflicting theories on where the outbreak originated. It is believed around 500 million people were infected, with about 50 million dying, making this one of the deadliest pandemics. The fatality rate was so high compared to usual because the victims’ immune systems overreacted and caused a cytokine storm which damaged the lungs. Since many of the deaths were caused by pneumonia, it was nicknamed ‘purple death’ because of how patients’ skin would turn purple from suffocation.

Nursery Rhyme:

Like many other diseases, the flu had its own nursery rhyme, which went as follows:

"I had a little bird,

Its name was Enza.

I opened the window,

And in-flu-enza."
